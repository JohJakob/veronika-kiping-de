moringa seed filter – product design

As the Moringa tree grows in Nepal, it's seeds can be well used to purify water in that coutry. The dried Moringa seeds, which can be extracted from the pods have to be dried and crushed before putting them in the moringa seed filter. The filter is a small device consisting of two parts, wich can be screwed together, allowing the cleaning of the filter as well as the change of the seed powder easy and quick. Three sieves control the flow rate of the water, giving the seeds enough time to absorb harmful bacteria. The filter can be screwed to jerry cans and with an adapter it can be screwed to regular water bottles which are easy to find in Nepal since there is no bottle return service. The moringa seed filter can be carried around due to it's small size and is therefore also inexpensive to produce. The only additional part Napalese people would require, is the Moringa tree, wich is a sustainable resource and whose parts can also be used to help with malnutrition and heath problems.

HSere are the winning projects of the Where There Is No Engineer competition 2017. This project was one of the 13 that made it through the Regional Finals and were presented in front of a number of judges.
